The Israeli Secret Services and the Struggle Against Terrorism

Ami Pedahzur · Columbia University Press, 2009

About the Book

This scholarly work examines Israel's counterterrorism strategies from 1948 onward. Pedahzur argues that Israel's reliance on elite intelligence units and offensive operations—including targeted killings—has proven less effective than defensive measures. The author analyzes specific historical cases including hostage rescues, the Lebanon wars, Palestinian territories, and Hezbollah operations.

Table of Contents

The book contains ten chapters covering the emergence of counterterrorism doctrine, the defensive model, hostage rescue, Lebanon, the West Bank and Gaza, Iran and Hezbollah, new rivals, elusive enemies, the Second Lebanon War, and fighting terrorism overall.
