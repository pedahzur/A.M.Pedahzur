About

My research asks a central question: how do states and non-state actors use organized violence, and what does that tell us about power, ideology, and strategy? Over three decades I have studied suicide terrorism, religious and ethno-nationalist extremism, and the evolution of special operations forces — work that has taken me from IDF archives to field interviews across three continents.

I direct the Chaikin Institute for Geostrategy at the University of Haifa and hold a professorship in Geostrategic Studies. Before returning to Israel I spent two decades at the University of Texas at Austin, where I built an interdisciplinary research program at the intersection of security studies and computational methods. My books have been published by Oxford University Press, Columbia University Press, Cambridge University Press, and Routledge.
