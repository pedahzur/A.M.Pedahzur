The Triumph of Israel's Radical Right

Ami Pedahzur · Oxford University Press, 2012

About the Book

This book provides an authoritative analysis of the radical right's ascendance to the heights of Israeli politics, focusing on the radical right's institutional networks and how the movement has been able to expand its influence over policy making.

After analyzing what the radical right believes in, the book explains how mainstream Israeli policies like "the law of return" have nurtured their nativism and authoritarian tendencies, then traces the right's steady expansion and mutation from the early days of the state to recent times.

The closing chapter contends that a two-state solution is no longer viable and that the vision of the radical rabbi Meir Kahane, who was a fringe figure while alive, has triumphed.

Scope

The book documents the rise of the radical right in Israeli politics and its impact on democratic institutions. It traces institutional networks, settler movements, and the political infrastructure that transformed fringe ideology into mainstream governance.
