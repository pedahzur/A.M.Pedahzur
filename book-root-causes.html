Root Causes of Suicide Terrorism: The Globalization of Martyrdom

Edited by Ami Pedahzur · Routledge, 2006

About the Book

This edited volume brings together leading scholars from political science, psychology, sociology, and religious studies to examine the root causes of suicide terrorism. Moving beyond single-factor explanations, the contributors analyze individual motivations, organizational strategies, and the broader societal conditions that give rise to this form of political violence.

The volume addresses suicide terrorism as a global phenomenon, examining cases from the Middle East, Sri Lanka, Chechnya, and beyond, and exploring how the tactic has been adopted and adapted across different cultural and political contexts.

Scope

Contributors explore how globalization has facilitated the diffusion of suicide terrorism as a tactic, examining the interplay between local grievances and transnational networks. The volume provides both theoretical frameworks and empirical case studies, making it a foundational text for understanding the phenomenon's spread.
