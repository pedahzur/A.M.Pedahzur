Suicide Terrorism

Ami Pedahzur · Polity Press, 2005

About the Book

Suicide terrorism in its modern form made its first appearance in Lebanon in the early 1980s. Over the last quarter century, terrorist attacks perpetrated by suicide bombers have spread to many corners of the world. This book asks whether this devastating phenomenon can be attributed to a specific religion or culture, what causes and motivations lead ordinary people to embark upon suicide attacks, and how potential bombers are trained for their mission.

Drawing on a variety of sources, the book explores the use of human bombs in Lebanon, Israel, Sri Lanka, Turkey, Chechnya, Iraq, and the attacks of Al-Qaeda and the global jihad. It is the only book to offer such an in-depth, up-to-date, cross-cultural analysis of suicide terrorism in the twenty-first century.

Key Features

The book explores who becomes a suicide bomber and how these "martyrs" are recruited and socialized by terrorist organizations. It provides new statistical data about suicide bombings and includes original interview material with key figures from terrorist groups.
