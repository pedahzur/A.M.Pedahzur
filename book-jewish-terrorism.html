Jewish Terrorism in Israel

Ami Pedahzur and Arie Perliger · Columbia University Press, 2009

About the Book

The authors examine violence through a historical lens spanning from ancient Palestine to contemporary Israel. They propose that religious terrorism emerges when totalistic communities perceive external threats, causing leaders' rhetoric and group cohesion to drive members toward violent responses.

Historical examples include the ancient sicarii, mid-twentieth-century independence movements, and the 1995 assassination of Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin by Yigal Amir. Part of the Columbia Studies in Terrorism and Irregular Warfare series.
